Section 01

Brand Overview

Mission

Treasury.gov serves as the primary digital presence for the U.S. Department of the Treasury, providing Americans with access to critical financial information, services, and resources. Our website must convey the authority and trustworthiness of a cabinet-level federal agency while remaining accessible to all citizens.

Brand Values

Value Expression
Authority The gravitas of a cabinet-level federal agency
Sophistication Premium, refined visual treatment
Trust Serious, reliable government presence
Modernity Contemporary design while respecting tradition

Target Audiences

  • General Public - Citizens seeking Treasury services and information
  • Financial Sector - Banks, investors, and financial professionals
  • Media - Journalists covering economic policy and Treasury announcements
  • Government Partners - Other agencies and international partners

  • Respect reduced motion: All animations disabled when prefers-reduced-motion: reduce is set

Typography Principles

  • Positive letter-spacing: Use +0.01em to +0.03em on headlines for editorial elegance. Never use negative tracking.
  • Tight line-height: Display and headline text uses 1.1-1.2 for impact. Body text uses 1.6 for readability.
  • Scale with viewport: Use CSS clamp() for responsive typography that maintains proportion.
  • Hero headlines at 120px+: Display text should command attention at desktop viewport widths.

Section 04

Brand Voice

Personality Traits

Trait Description
Authoritative Speaks with the weight and expertise of a cabinet-level agency
Trustworthy Reliable, accurate, and transparent
Clear Plain language, accessible to all
Professional Serious, dignified, appropriate
Helpful Service-oriented, user-focused

Voice Examples

Don't

"Pursuant to the provisions of Section 6402 of the Internal Revenue Code, the Department of the Treasury hereby notifies affected taxpayers that offset procedures may be initiated with respect to outstanding federal debts."

Do

"If you owe federal debt, your tax refund may be reduced to pay it. This is called a tax refund offset. Learn what debts can cause an offset and how to check if you're affected."

Tone Guidelines

Do

  • Use plain language
  • Be direct and concise
  • Address the reader as "you"
  • Provide specific numbers and dates
  • Explain acronyms on first use
  • Use active voice

Don't

  • Use bureaucratic jargon
  • Bury the lead in paragraphs
  • Use passive voice excessively
  • Make vague promises
  • Assume familiarity with government terms
  • Use superlatives or marketing speak

Tone Matrix

Treasury's voice remains consistent, but tone shifts based on context:

Context Tone Example
Crisis Calm, direct, reassuring "Treasury is taking immediate action to stabilize markets."
Policy Authoritative, factual, precise "The new regulation requires financial institutions to..."
Educational Approachable, clear, helpful "Here's how the tax credit works and who qualifies."
Service Supportive, patient, solution-oriented "We can help you resolve this issue. Here are your options."
Success Measured pride, factual celebration "This program has helped 2 million Americans save for retirement."

Word Lists

Preferred Terms

  • Use "help" not "assist"
  • Use "start" not "commence"
  • Use "end" not "terminate"
  • Use "get" not "obtain"
  • Use "need" not "require"
  • Use "about" not "approximately"
  • Use "before" not "prior to"
  • Use "after" not "subsequent to"

Words to Avoid

  • Pursuant to
  • Herein, hereby, herewith
  • Aforementioned
  • Effectuate
  • Utilize (use "use")
  • Facilitate
  • Promulgate
  • Notwithstanding

Messaging Hierarchy

Level Content
Tagline Serving the American people and strengthening the economy
Value Proposition Treasury maintains a strong economy, manages government finances, and combats threats to our financial systems
Proof Points $X trillion in government payments processed annually; XX sanctions programs protecting national security; Direct impact on every American's financial life

Boilerplate Library

50 Words

The U.S. Department of the Treasury works to maintain a strong economy, manage government finances, and protect the integrity of the financial system. Treasury serves all Americans through programs that promote economic growth, collect taxes fairly, and safeguard against financial threats.

100 Words

The U.S. Department of the Treasury is the executive agency responsible for promoting economic prosperity and ensuring the financial security of the United States. Treasury operates and maintains systems that are critical to the nation's financial infrastructure, including currency production, revenue collection, and disbursement of government payments. The Department works to strengthen national security through targeted financial sanctions, combat financial crimes, and promote conditions for economic growth and job creation both at home and abroad. Treasury serves every American through programs that impact their daily financial lives.

Logo System

Treasury Seal

The Treasury seal is the official mark of the Department. It must be used according to federal guidelines and treated with respect. Use the full seal at larger sizes where detail is visible. At smaller sizes (below ~140px), the outer ring text becomes too small to read (<10px), so use the simplified version.

Clear Space

Maintain clear space around the seal equal to at least 1/4 of the seal's width on all sides. No other elements should intrude into this space.

Logo Don'ts

  • Don't place on busy backgrounds
  • Don't rotate the seal

Section 07

Photography

Photography Style

Treasury photography should convey authority, professionalism, and trustworthiness while remaining accessible and engaging.

Photography Archetypes

Archetype Description Use Cases
The Guardian Treasury building, vaults, architecture that conveys protection and permanence Homepage heroes, section backgrounds, institutional pages
The Leader Secretary and officials in action - testimony, meetings, press conferences News releases, leadership pages, speeches
The Impact Americans affected by Treasury policies - beneficiaries, communities Program pages, success stories, citizen services
The Heritage Historical imagery - founding documents, past Secretaries, historic moments About pages, history sections, commemorative content

Subject Guidelines

Subject Direction
People Diverse, professional, engaged in meaningful work
Buildings Treasury building, government architecture, dignified settings
Documents Official documents, currency, financial imagery
Events Press conferences, meetings, official ceremonies

Technical Requirements

Specification Requirement
Resolution Minimum 2400px on longest edge for hero images, 1200px for cards
Aspect Ratios 16:9 (hero), 4:3 (cards), 1:1 (thumbnails)
File Formats JPG for photographs, PNG for graphics with transparency
Color Space sRGB for web, Adobe RGB for print

Image Treatment

  • Saturation: Reduce by 10-15% from original for sophisticated feel
  • Color Cast: Slight cool/navy shift to align with brand palette
  • Contrast: Slightly elevated for definition without harshness
  • Prohibited: Heavy filters, artificial vignettes, stock photo watermarks

Photography Do's and Don'ts

Do

  • Show people in authentic work contexts
  • Capture candid moments of leadership
  • Feature diverse Americans as beneficiaries
  • Use Treasury building as visual anchor
  • Include environmental context (40-60% of frame)

Don't

  • Use generic stock photography
  • Stage handshakes or "diverse team pointing at laptop"
  • Show posed confidence poses (arms crossed)
  • Use images that could represent any agency
  • Apply heavy filters or unnatural color grading

Human Photography Guidelines

Treasury imagery featuring people must feel authentic and authoritative, never like generic corporate stock photos.

When to Show Humans

Show humans when:
  • Demonstrating Treasury IMPACT on real Americans
  • Secretary/leadership in official capacity
  • Historical context (Hamilton, past Secretaries)
  • Service delivery moments with citizens
Do NOT show humans when:
  • Abstract concepts (use architecture instead)
  • Generic "working" scenes
  • Decorative filler imagery
  • Images that could be ANY government agency

How Humans Should Look

Principle Description
Caught in Action Never posed looking at camera - documentary style
Environmental Context Treasury building, hearing room, community visible (40-60% of frame)
Dignified Formal attire appropriate to context, never casual
Purposeful Clearly doing something meaningful, not "thinking" or "collaborating"
Authentic Lighting Natural or documentary-style, never studio-lit

Anti-Patterns (Never Do These)

  • NO handshakes
  • NO pointing at screens/whiteboards
  • NO "diverse team laughing together"
  • NO arms crossed "confidence pose"
  • NO stock-photo eye contact with camera
  • NO generic office backgrounds
  • NO obviously staged "candid" moments
  • NO suits walking toward camera
  • NO thumbs up, high fives, celebrations

Section 08

Brand in Use

Application Guidelines

Apply the Treasury brand consistently across all digital touchpoints using the patterns documented in this guide.

Page Type Guidelines

Homepage Hero

  • Full-width navy background
  • Playfair Display headline
  • Gold primary CTA button
  • Subtle gold accent lines

Content Pages

  • Stone (#E8E6E0) background for readability
  • Navy header bar with white text
  • Inter body text at 16px
  • Gold category labels

Data & Dashboards

  • Dark navy background
  • Gold for data highlights
  • Subtle card borders
  • Clear data hierarchy

Forms & Services

  • Clean white/stone background
  • Gold submit buttons
  • Clear field labels
  • Progress indicators

Key Patterns

  • Navy backgrounds with white text for primary sections
  • Gold accents for calls-to-action and highlights
  • Stone backgrounds for contrast sections
  • Consistent use of eyebrow + heading + gold underline
  • Card-based layouts with subtle borders
