## Daily Treasury Bill Rates

Treasury bill rates represent yields on short-term Treasury securities with maturities of one year or less.

### Types of Rates

#### Bank Discount Rate
The traditional quote convention for Treasury bills:
- Quoted on a discount basis
- 360-day year convention
- Used in auction announcements

#### Coupon Equivalent (Investment Rate)
Also known as the bond-equivalent yield:
- Comparable to coupon-bearing securities
- 365/366-day year convention
- Useful for comparing across securities

### Available Maturities

Daily rates are published for:
- 4 week (1 month)
- 8 week (2 month)
- 13 week (3 month)
- 17 week (4 month)
- 26 week (6 month)
- 52 week (1 year)

### About Treasury Bills

Treasury bills are:
- Sold at a discount to face value
- Mature at full face value
- Zero-coupon securities
- Highly liquid and considered risk-free

### Data Availability

- **Updates**: Daily (business days)
- **Source**: Secondary market trading
- **History**: Historical data available
- **Format**: Text and downloadable

### Uses

Treasury bill rates are used as:
- Short-term benchmark rates
- Money market fund benchmarks
- Cash management references
- Discount rate calculations
