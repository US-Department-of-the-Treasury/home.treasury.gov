## Daily Treasury Long-Term Rates

The Treasury long-term rate is derived from the yield on Treasury securities with remaining maturities of 10 years or more.

### Long-Term Average Rate

This rate represents:
- Unweighted average of bid yields on all outstanding Treasury bonds
- Securities with 10+ years remaining to maturity
- Excludes callable bonds
- Calculated daily from secondary market quotes

### Historical Context

The long-term average rate has historically been used for:
- Long-term investment benchmarking
- Economic analysis
- Regulatory and statutory references
- Historical comparisons

### Extrapolation Factor

For maturities beyond available data, Treasury publishes:
- Long-term extrapolated rate
- Useful for very long-term discounting
- Based on modeling beyond 30-year point

### Data Availability

- **Updates**: Daily (business days)
- **Source**: Secondary market trading
- **History**: Historical data available
- **Format**: Text and downloadable formats

### Uses

Long-term rates are used for:
- Pension discounting
- Insurance liability valuation
- Long-term project analysis
- Economic research
