## Daily Treasury Par Yield Curve Rates

The Treasury par yield curve rates are derived from Treasury securities trading in the secondary market. These rates are commonly referred to as "Constant Maturity Treasury" (CMT) rates.

### What Are Par Yield Curve Rates?

The par yield curve represents yields at which Treasury securities would trade if they were priced at par (100 cents on the dollar). These rates are interpolated from the daily yield curve and are widely used as benchmark rates.

### Available Maturities

Daily rates are published for the following maturities:
- 1 month
- 2 month
- 3 month
- 4 month
- 6 month
- 1 year
- 2 year
- 3 year
- 5 year
- 7 year
- 10 year
- 20 year
- 30 year

### Data Availability

- **Updates**: Daily (business days)
- **Time**: Typically available by late afternoon Eastern Time
- **History**: Historical data available for download
- **Format**: Text, XML, and downloadable formats

### Uses of Par Yield Curve Rates

These rates are used for:
- Benchmarking fixed income investments
- Mortgage rate pricing
- Financial modeling and analysis
- Corporate bond spread calculations
- Economic research
