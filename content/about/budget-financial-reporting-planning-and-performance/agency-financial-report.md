The U.S. Department of the Treasury publishes an annual Agency Financial Report (AFR) that provides a comprehensive overview of the Department's financial performance and condition.

### Purpose of the AFR

The Agency Financial Report fulfills Treasury's responsibility to:
- Present audited financial statements
- Report on internal controls
- Discuss financial management achievements and challenges
- Demonstrate accountability to Congress and the American public

### Contents of the AFR

#### Management's Discussion and Analysis
- Mission and organizational overview
- Performance highlights
- Financial highlights
- Analysis of financial statements
- Systems, controls, and legal compliance

#### Financial Section
- Independent auditor's report
- Consolidated financial statements
- Notes to the financial statements
- Required supplementary information

#### Other Information
- Inspector General reports
- Management challenges
- Payment integrity reporting
- Civil monetary penalty adjustments

### Treasury's Financial Responsibilities

As the steward of America's economic and financial systems, Treasury manages:
- Collection of federal revenue
- Disbursement of federal payments
- Management of federal debt
- Production of currency and coins
- Enforcement of economic sanctions

### Accessing the Reports

Agency Financial Reports are available on:
- Treasury's website
- [USASpending.gov](https://www.usaspending.gov/)
- Government Accountability Office archives
