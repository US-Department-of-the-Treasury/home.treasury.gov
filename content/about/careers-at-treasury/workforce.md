The U.S. Department of the Treasury is home to a diverse, talented, and dedicated workforce committed to serving the American people.

### Workforce Overview

The Treasury Department employs approximately 100,000 people across its headquarters offices and bureaus. The workforce includes:

- Economists and financial analysts
- Accountants and auditors
- Attorneys and legal professionals
- Law enforcement officers
- Information technology specialists
- Administrative professionals
- Scientists and engineers
- Customer service representatives

### Our Largest Bureau

The Internal Revenue Service (IRS) is the largest bureau within Treasury, employing the majority of Treasury's workforce to administer the nation's tax system.

### Workforce Demographics

Treasury is committed to building a diverse and inclusive workforce that reflects the American public we serve:

- **Diversity** - Employees from all backgrounds and regions
- **Inclusion** - Programs to ensure all voices are heard
- **Accessibility** - Accommodations for employees with disabilities
- **Equity** - Fair treatment in hiring, promotion, and development

### Workforce Locations

Treasury employees work across the United States:

- **Washington, D.C.** - Headquarters offices
- **Regional Offices** - Major metropolitan areas
- **Field Offices** - Cities and towns nationwide
- **U.S. Mint Facilities** - Philadelphia, Denver, San Francisco, West Point
- **BEP Facilities** - Washington, D.C. and Fort Worth, Texas

### Employee Engagement

Treasury is committed to employee engagement through:

- Employee surveys and feedback
- Town halls and communication forums
- Recognition programs
- Work-life programs
- Professional development
