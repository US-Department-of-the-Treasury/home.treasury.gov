The U.S. Department of the Treasury's mission is to maintain a strong economy and create economic and job opportunities by promoting the conditions that enable economic growth and stability at home and abroad, strengthen national security by combating threats and protecting the integrity of the financial system, and manage the U.S. Government's finances and resources effectively.

Treasury's mission highlights its role as the steward of U.S. economic and financial systems, and as an influential participant in the world economy.

## Overview

The Treasury Department is the executive agency responsible for promoting economic prosperity and ensuring the financial security of the United States. The Department is responsible for a wide range of activities such as advising the President on economic and financial issues, encouraging sustainable economic growth, and fostering improved governance in financial institutions.

The Department of the Treasury operates and maintains systems that are critical to the nation's financial infrastructure, such as the production of coin and currency, the disbursement of payments to the American public, revenue collection, and the borrowing of funds necessary to run the federal government.

The Department works with other federal agencies, foreign governments, and international financial institutions to encourage global economic growth, raise standards of living, and to the extent possible, predict and prevent economic and financial crises.

The Treasury Department also performs a critical and far-reaching role in enhancing national security by implementing economic sanctions against foreign threats to the U.S., identifying and targeting the financial support networks of national security threats, and improving the safeguards of our financial systems.

## Core Functions

Treasury's core functions include:

1. **Managing Federal Finances** - Collecting revenue and making payments, managing federal finances, and operating the government's accounts
2. **Financial Infrastructure** - Producing currency and coinage, disbursing payments to the American public, and acting as the government's financial agent
3. **Economic Policy** - Advising the President on domestic and international economic policy
4. **Tax Policy** - Developing tax legislative proposals and enforcing federal tax laws
5. **National Security** - Implementing economic sanctions, combating financial crimes, and protecting the integrity of the financial system

## Leadership

The Department is administered by the Secretary of the Treasury, who is a member of the President's Cabinet and serves as the principal economic advisor to the President.
