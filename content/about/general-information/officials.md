## Secretary of the Treasury

The Secretary of the Treasury is the principal economic advisor to the President and plays a critical role in policy-making by bringing an economic and government financial policy perspective to issues facing the government. The Secretary is responsible for formulating and recommending domestic and international financial, economic, and tax policy; participating in the formulation of broad fiscal policies; and managing the public debt.

For information about the current Secretary, visit the [Secretary's biography page](/about/general-information/secretary).

## Deputy Secretary of the Treasury

The Deputy Secretary of the Treasury is the second-ranking official at the Department. The Deputy Secretary assists the Secretary in supervising the activities of the Department and is the chief operating officer, responsible for day-to-day management.

## Under Secretaries

The Department has four Under Secretaries who oversee major policy areas:

### Under Secretary for Domestic Finance
Advises and assists in areas of domestic finance, financial institutions, federal debt finance, financial regulation, and capital markets. Oversees the Bureau of the Fiscal Service and the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency.

### Under Secretary for International Affairs
Advises and assists on international monetary, financial, commercial, energy, and trade policies and programs. Leads Treasury's engagement with the IMF, World Bank, G-7, and G-20.

### Under Secretary for Terrorism and Financial Intelligence
Leads the Department's intelligence and enforcement efforts to combat financial crimes, including money laundering, terrorist financing, and the proliferation of weapons of mass destruction.

### Under Secretary for Management
Serves as the chief management and administrative officer, responsible for human resources, information technology, facilities, and other administrative functions.

## Assistant Secretaries

Assistant Secretaries lead specific policy and management areas within the Department, including:

- **Economic Policy** - Economic analysis and policy development
- **Tax Policy** - Tax legislation and regulation
- **Financial Institutions** - Banking and financial services policy
- **Financial Markets** - Debt management and government securities
- **Legislative Affairs** - Congressional relations
- **Public Affairs** - Communications and media relations
- **Management** - Administrative services and operations

## Inspectors General

- **Inspector General** - Conducts audits and investigations of Treasury programs and operations
- **Treasury Inspector General for Tax Administration (TIGTA)** - Oversight of IRS programs and operations
- **Special Inspector General for Pandemic Recovery (SIGPR)** - Oversight of pandemic relief programs
