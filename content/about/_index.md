The Department of the Treasury is the executive agency responsible for promoting economic prosperity and ensuring the financial security of the United States. Treasury operates and maintains systems that are critical to the nation's financial infrastructure, including producing currency and coinage, disbursing payments to the American public, collecting taxes, and borrowing funds necessary to run the federal government.
