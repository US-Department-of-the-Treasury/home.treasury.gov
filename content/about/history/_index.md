The Department of the Treasury was established by an Act of Congress in 1789. The Department is administered by the Secretary of the Treasury, who is a member of the President's Cabinet.

Alexander Hamilton was appointed the first Secretary of the Treasury on September 11, 1789, and served until January 31, 1795.
