## Founding

The Department of the Treasury was established by Act of Congress on September 2, 1789, to manage government revenue.

## Early Years

Alexander Hamilton, the first Secretary of the Treasury, established:

- The nation's first central bank
- A system for managing the national debt
- Customs duties as a primary revenue source
- The U.S. Mint

## Civil War Era

During the Civil War, Treasury:

- Introduced the first income tax
- Issued paper currency ("greenbacks")
- Established the national banking system
- Created the Bureau of Internal Revenue (now IRS)

## Modern Era

Today, Treasury:

- Manages the nation's finances
- Administers economic sanctions
- Produces currency and coins
- Collects federal taxes
- Enforces financial crimes laws
