## Notable Secretaries

### Alexander Hamilton (1789-1795)
The first Secretary of the Treasury, Hamilton established the financial foundation of the United States, including the First Bank of the United States, assumption of state debts, and the U.S. Mint.

### Albert Gallatin (1801-1814)
The longest-serving Secretary (nearly 13 years), Gallatin significantly reduced the national debt while funding the Louisiana Purchase.

### Salmon P. Chase (1861-1864)
Financed the Civil War through innovative measures including the issuance of "greenbacks" (paper currency) and the establishment of the national banking system.

### Andrew W. Mellon (1921-1932)
Served under three presidents (Harding, Coolidge, Hoover) and oversaw significant tax reductions during the prosperous 1920s.

### Henry Morgenthau Jr. (1934-1945)
The second-longest serving Secretary, Morgenthau managed Treasury through the Great Depression and World War II, including financing the war effort and planning for post-war international monetary institutions.

### Janet L. Yellen (2021-2025)
The first woman to serve as Secretary of the Treasury, having previously served as Chair of the Federal Reserve.
