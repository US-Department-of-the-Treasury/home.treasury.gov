## The Treasury Building

The Treasury Building is one of the oldest federal buildings in Washington, D.C., and is a National Historic Landmark. Located at 1500 Pennsylvania Avenue NW, directly east of the White House, the building has served as the headquarters of the U.S. Department of the Treasury since 1836.

### History

#### Construction
The current Treasury Building was constructed in stages from 1836 to 1869. The original architect was Robert Mills, who designed the east wing in the Greek Revival style. The building was expanded over three decades with the addition of the south, west, and north wings.

#### Architectural Significance
- **Style**: Greek Revival with Ionic columns
- **Material**: Granite exterior
- **Features**: The colonnade on the east wing consists of 30 columns, each 36 feet tall
- **Status**: Designated a National Historic Landmark in 1972

#### Historical Events
- The building has witnessed many significant moments in American history
- It served as a barracks during the Civil War
- President Andrew Johnson used offices in the building after Lincoln's assassination
- The Cash Room, with its seven types of marble, has hosted presidential inaugural receptions

### The Cash Room

The Cash Room is one of the most magnificent spaces in the Treasury Building. Completed in 1869, it features:
- Seven varieties of marble from Italy, Belgium, and Vermont
- Bronze chandeliers and railings
- Ornate coffered ceiling
- Originally used for financial transactions, now used for ceremonial events

### Tours

Public tours of the Treasury Building are available by reservation. Tours include:
- The historic Cash Room
- The restored Andrew Johnson Suite
- The Secretary's Conference Room
- Historical exhibits and displays

**To request a tour**, contact your member of Congress or email the Treasury tour coordinator.

### Location

**Address**: 1500 Pennsylvania Avenue NW, Washington, DC 20220
