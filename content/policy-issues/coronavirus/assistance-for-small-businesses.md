## Assistance for Small Businesses

The U.S. Department of the Treasury administered and supported multiple programs to help small businesses survive and recover from the COVID-19 pandemic.

### Paycheck Protection Program (PPP)

PPP provided forgivable loans to small businesses to maintain payroll:

#### Program Features
- Loans up to 2.5 times monthly payroll
- Funds for payroll, rent, utilities, and mortgage interest
- Loan forgiveness if used for eligible expenses
- Administered through SBA-approved lenders

#### Impact
- Millions of loans to small businesses
- Hundreds of billions in forgivable loans
- Jobs retained across the economy

### Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFI) Fund

Treasury's CDFI Fund deployed resources to underserved communities:
- Emergency Capital Investment Program
- CDFI Rapid Response Program
- Support for minority and underserved businesses

### Employee Retention Credit

Tax credits for employers keeping workers on payroll:
- Refundable payroll tax credit
- Available to businesses with revenue declines
- Quarterly credit based on qualified wages

### State Small Business Credit Initiative (SSBCI)

American Rescue Plan provided $10 billion to states for:
- Small business lending programs
- Venture capital investments
- Technical assistance
- Focus on underserved entrepreneurs

[Learn more about SSBCI](/policy-issues/small-business-programs/state-small-business-credit-initiative-ssbci/)

### Economic Injury Disaster Loans (EIDL)

SBA provided low-interest loans and advances for:
- Working capital
- Fixed debts
- Payroll and accounts payable
- Other operating expenses
