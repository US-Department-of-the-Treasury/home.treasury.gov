The U.S. Department of the Treasury administered programs to support critical American industries severely impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic.

### Airline Industry Support

Treasury provided payroll support to the aviation industry:

#### Payroll Support Program (PSP)
Multiple rounds of payroll support for:
- Passenger air carriers
- Cargo air carriers
- Airline contractors

#### Program Requirements
- Funds used for employee wages and benefits
- Restrictions on layoffs and furloughs
- Limits on executive compensation
- Stock buyback prohibitions

#### PSP Rounds
- PSP1 (CARES Act) - $32 billion
- PSP2 (December 2020) - $15 billion
- PSP3 (American Rescue Plan) - $15 billion

### National Security Industries

CARES Act authorized loans and loan guarantees for:
- Businesses critical to national security
- Industries essential to maintaining U.S. capabilities
- Mid-size businesses affected by COVID-19

### Loans and Equity Investments

Treasury made direct loans to certain critical companies:
- Loans secured by appropriate collateral
- Equity warrants or other compensation
- Strict terms protecting taxpayer interests

### Special Inspector General

The Special Inspector General for Pandemic Recovery (SIGPR) provides oversight of:
- Economic stabilization programs
- Loans to large businesses
- Compliance with program requirements

[Visit SIGPR](https://www.sigpr.gov/)

### Program Outcomes

Treasury's industry assistance:
- Preserved jobs in critical sectors
- Maintained essential services
- Protected taxpayer investments through strong oversight
- Supported economic recovery
