## Assistance for American Families and Workers

The U.S. Department of the Treasury administered multiple programs to provide economic relief to American families and workers impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic.

### Economic Impact Payments

Treasury, in coordination with the IRS, distributed Economic Impact Payments (stimulus payments) to eligible Americans:

#### Payment Rounds
- **First Round (CARES Act)** - Up to $1,200 per adult, $500 per child
- **Second Round (December 2020)** - Up to $600 per person
- **Third Round (American Rescue Plan)** - Up to $1,400 per person

#### Eligibility
Payments were based on:
- Adjusted gross income
- Filing status
- Number of dependents
- Citizenship and residency status

### Enhanced Unemployment Insurance

Federal programs supplemented state unemployment benefits:
- Extended benefit duration
- Pandemic Unemployment Assistance for self-employed and gig workers
- Federal Pandemic Unemployment Compensation (additional weekly payments)

### Child Tax Credit Advance Payments

The American Rescue Plan expanded the Child Tax Credit and provided advance monthly payments:
- Up to $300 per month for children under 6
- Up to $250 per month for children 6-17
- Payments from July through December 2021

### Emergency Rental Assistance

Treasury administered Emergency Rental Assistance to help tenants and landlords:
- Rental arrears and future payments
- Utility payments
- Housing stability services

### Homeowner Assistance Fund

The Homeowner Assistance Fund helped homeowners with:
- Mortgage payment assistance
- Property tax relief
- Utility assistance
- Insurance payments
