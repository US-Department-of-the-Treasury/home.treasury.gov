## Section 1603 Program

The Section 1603 program provided direct payments to renewable energy project developers in lieu of investment tax credits during the financial crisis period.

### Program Overview

Section 1603 of the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009:
- Provided cash grants for renewable energy projects
- Offered payment in lieu of investment or production tax credits
- Addressed reduced tax appetite during financial crisis
- Accelerated clean energy development

### Eligible Technologies

The program covered:
- Solar (thermal and photovoltaic)
- Wind
- Geothermal
- Fuel cells
- Microturbines
- Combined heat and power
- Small wind
- Geothermal heat pumps
- Municipal solid waste

### Payment Amount

Grants were based on the eligible basis of the property:
- 30% for solar, fuel cells, wind, and small wind
- 10% for other eligible technologies

### Program Status

The 1603 program:
- Accepted applications for projects placed in service through 2011
- Construction start deadline extended to 2012
- Program has wound down; no new applications accepted

### Program Impact

The 1603 program supported:
- Thousands of renewable energy projects
- Billions of dollars in private investment
- Significant clean energy capacity
- Jobs in clean energy sector

### Related Programs

Current clean energy incentives include:
- Investment Tax Credit (ITC)
- Production Tax Credit (PTC)
- Clean energy provisions in the Inflation Reduction Act
- [CDFI Fund Clean Energy Programs](https://www.cdfifund.gov/)
