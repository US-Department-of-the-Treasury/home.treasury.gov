## Federal Insurance Office

The Federal Insurance Office (FIO), established by the Dodd-Frank Act, monitors the insurance industry and represents the United States on prudential aspects of international insurance matters.

### Mission

FIO monitors all aspects of the insurance industry and represents U.S. interests in international insurance forums.

### Key Responsibilities

#### Monitoring the Insurance Sector
- Identifying issues that could contribute to systemic crisis
- Monitoring access to affordable insurance
- Analyzing and reporting on the insurance industry

#### International Representation
- Participating in international insurance bodies
- Negotiating covered agreements
- Representing U.S. insurance interests abroad

#### Advisory Role
- Advising the Secretary on insurance policy
- Consulting with state regulators
- Coordinating federal insurance policy

### Reports

FIO publishes reports on:
- Annual report on the insurance industry
- Climate-related financial risks in insurance
- Modernization of insurance regulation
- Access to insurance coverage

### Covered Agreements

FIO negotiates covered agreements with foreign jurisdictions on:
- Reinsurance collateral requirements
- Group supervision of internationally active insurers
- Exchange of information between supervisors

### Terrorism Risk Insurance

FIO administers the Terrorism Risk Insurance Program (TRIP):
- Provides federal backstop for terrorism insurance
- Certifies acts of terrorism
- Manages recoupment from insurers

### Coordination

FIO coordinates with:
- State insurance regulators
- National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC)
- International Association of Insurance Supervisors (IAIS)
- FSOC member agencies
