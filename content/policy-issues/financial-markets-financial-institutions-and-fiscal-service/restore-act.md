## RESTORE Act

The Resources and Ecosystems Sustainability, Tourist Opportunities, and Revived Economies of the Gulf Coast States Act (RESTORE Act) directs Clean Water Act penalties from the Deepwater Horizon oil spill to Gulf Coast restoration.

### Overview

The RESTORE Act establishes the Gulf Coast Restoration Trust Fund, which receives:
- 80% of Clean Water Act civil and administrative penalties from the Deepwater Horizon spill
- Funds dedicated to Gulf Coast ecological and economic restoration

### Trust Fund Components

The Trust Fund is divided into five components:

#### 1. Direct Component (35%)
Equal allocation to five Gulf Coast states:
- Alabama
- Florida
- Louisiana
- Mississippi
- Texas

States use funds for eligible activities including:
- Coastal restoration and protection
- Mitigation of damage to ecosystems and wildlife
- Economic and workforce development
- Tourism promotion

#### 2. Comprehensive Plan Component (30%)
Funds for the Gulf Coast Ecosystem Restoration Council to:
- Develop and implement a comprehensive restoration plan
- Fund projects that restore the Gulf Coast ecosystem

#### 3. Spill Impact Component (30%)
Distributed based on a formula considering:
- Proportionate impact from the spill
- Directed to areas most affected

#### 4. NOAA Science Program (2.5%)
Funds for the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration for:
- Gulf science, observation, and monitoring
- Research to support restoration

#### 5. Centers of Excellence (2.5%)
Establishes research programs focusing on:
- Science-based Gulf restoration
- Coastal sustainability research

### Treasury's Role

Treasury:
- Manages the Gulf Coast Restoration Trust Fund
- Invests and disburses funds
- Reports on fund status
