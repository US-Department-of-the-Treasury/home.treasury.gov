The Secretary of the Treasury serves as the Managing Trustee of the Social Security and Medicare Trust Funds, a role that underscores Treasury's central responsibility in ensuring the financial integrity of these critical programs.

### The Secretary's Role as Managing Trustee

As Managing Trustee, the Secretary of the Treasury:

- Chairs the Board of Trustees for both Social Security and Medicare
- Oversees the annual reports on the financial status of the trust funds
- Works with other trustees to ensure sound stewardship of program finances
- Provides policy recommendations for long-term sustainability

### Annual Trustees Reports

Each year, the Trustees release reports on the financial status of:

- **Old-Age and Survivors Insurance (OASI) Trust Fund**
- **Disability Insurance (DI) Trust Fund**
- **Hospital Insurance (HI) Trust Fund** (Medicare Part A)
- **Supplementary Medical Insurance (SMI) Trust Fund** (Medicare Parts B and D)

These reports provide projections of the financial status of the trust funds over the next 75 years.

### Treasury's Economic Analysis

Treasury's Office of Economic Policy provides analysis on:

- Long-term projections for program finances
- Economic and demographic assumptions underlying trust fund projections
- Policy options to address long-term sustainability
- Impact of economic conditions on program finances
