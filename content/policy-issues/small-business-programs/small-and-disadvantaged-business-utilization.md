The Office of Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization (OSDBU) helps small businesses access contracting opportunities with the U.S. Department of the Treasury.

### Mission

OSDBU promotes the use of small businesses in Treasury contracting by:
- Advocating for small business participation
- Providing outreach and education
- Monitoring small business goals
- Connecting businesses with opportunities

### Small Business Categories

Treasury tracks and promotes contracting with:
- **Small Businesses** - Meet SBA size standards
- **Small Disadvantaged Businesses (SDB)** - Socially and economically disadvantaged
- **Women-Owned Small Businesses (WOSB)** - At least 51% women-owned
- **Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Businesses (SDVOSB)** - Owned by service-disabled veterans
- **HUBZone Businesses** - Located in Historically Underutilized Business Zones
- **8(a) Businesses** - Participating in SBA 8(a) development program

### How to Do Business with Treasury

#### Get Registered
1. Obtain a DUNS number
2. Register in SAM.gov (System for Award Management)
3. Complete SBA certification if applicable

#### Find Opportunities
- Search [SAM.gov](https://sam.gov/) for solicitations
- Monitor Treasury bureau procurement forecasts
- Attend industry days and outreach events

#### Market Your Business
- Respond to market research requests
- Attend vendor outreach events
- Meet with OSDBU and contracting officers

### Treasury Bureaus

Each Treasury bureau has procurement operations:
- Internal Revenue Service
- Bureau of the Fiscal Service
- U.S. Mint
- Bureau of Engraving and Printing
- Others

### Contact OSDBU

For assistance with small business opportunities at Treasury, contact the Office of Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization.
