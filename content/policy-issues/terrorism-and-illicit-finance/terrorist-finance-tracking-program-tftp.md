The Terrorist Finance Tracking Program is a critical counterterrorism tool that uses financial data to identify, track, and pursue terrorists and their networks.

### Program Overview

The TFTP:
- Identifies and tracks terrorist financing networks
- Provides leads to law enforcement and intelligence agencies
- Supports designation of terrorist financiers
- Enhances understanding of terrorist financial methods

### Legal Authority

The program operates under:
- Subpoenas issued under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA)
- Executive orders addressing national emergencies
- Oversight from multiple government branches

### Safeguards and Oversight

Robust safeguards protect privacy and civil liberties:
- Independent oversight mechanisms
- Strict data handling procedures
- Purpose limitations on use
- Regular audits and reviews

### U.S.-EU TFTP Agreement

The United States and European Union have an agreement governing:
- Transfer of financial messaging data
- Data protection requirements
- Oversight by European officials
- Review of program effectiveness

### Program Effectiveness

The TFTP has contributed to:
- Identifying terrorist cells
- Disrupting terrorist plots
- Supporting sanctions designations
- Prosecuting terrorist financiers

### Transparency

Treasury provides information about:
- Program legal basis
- Oversight mechanisms
- Privacy protections
- General program effectiveness
