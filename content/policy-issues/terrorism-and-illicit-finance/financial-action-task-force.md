The Financial Action Task Force is the global standard-setting body for anti-money laundering (AML) and combating the financing of terrorism (CFT). The U.S. Department of the Treasury represents the United States in FATF.

### About FATF

The FATF is an inter-governmental body that:
- Sets international AML/CFT standards
- Monitors members' compliance
- Studies money laundering and terrorist financing trends
- Identifies high-risk jurisdictions

### The FATF Recommendations

FATF's 40 Recommendations are the internationally recognized standards for:
- Legal systems and criminal justice
- Financial sector regulation
- Transparency and beneficial ownership
- International cooperation
- Terrorist financing and proliferation

### U.S. Participation

Treasury leads U.S. engagement with FATF:
- Participating in FATF plenary meetings
- Contributing to standard-setting
- Undergoing mutual evaluations
- Supporting technical assistance

### FATF Initiatives

#### Mutual Evaluations
FATF conducts peer reviews of member countries' AML/CFT systems to assess:
- Technical compliance with standards
- Effectiveness of implementation

#### High-Risk Jurisdictions
FATF identifies jurisdictions with strategic AML/CFT deficiencies:
- Countries under increased monitoring ("grey list")
- High-risk jurisdictions subject to calls for action ("black list")

#### Emerging Risks
FATF studies new threats including:
- Virtual assets and cryptocurrencies
- Trade-based money laundering
- Professional money laundering
- Environmental crime
