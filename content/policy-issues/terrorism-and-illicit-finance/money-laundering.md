## Money Laundering

The U.S. Department of the Treasury leads federal efforts to combat money laundering through policy development, regulation, and enforcement.

### What Is Money Laundering?

Money laundering is the process of concealing the origins of illegally obtained money, typically by passing it through a complex sequence of banking transfers or commercial transactions.

### Treasury's Role

#### Policy Development
Treasury develops anti-money laundering (AML) policy through:
- Rulemaking and regulations
- Interagency coordination
- International engagement
- Industry guidance

#### Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN)
[FinCEN](https://www.fincen.gov/) is Treasury's bureau responsible for:
- Administering the Bank Secrecy Act
- Collecting and analyzing financial intelligence
- Issuing regulations for financial institutions
- Sharing information with law enforcement

### Key Regulations

#### Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)
The BSA requires financial institutions to:
- Maintain AML programs
- File Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs)
- File Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
- Verify customer identity (Customer Due Diligence)

#### Beneficial Ownership Reporting
New requirements for companies to report beneficial ownership information to FinCEN enhance transparency and combat anonymous shell companies.

### Enforcement

Treasury combats money laundering through:
- Civil penalties against violators
- Coordination with law enforcement
- International cooperation
- Designation of money laundering jurisdictions

### International Cooperation

Treasury works with:
- [Financial Action Task Force (FATF)](/policy-issues/terrorism-and-illicit-finance/financial-action-task-force/)
- Egmont Group of Financial Intelligence Units
- Foreign government partners
- International financial institutions
