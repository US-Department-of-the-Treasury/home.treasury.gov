## Innovations in Financial Services

The U.S. Department of the Treasury monitors and shapes policy related to innovations in financial services, including fintech, digital assets, and emerging payment technologies.

### Areas of Focus

#### Financial Technology (Fintech)
Treasury examines how technology is transforming:
- Payment systems
- Lending and credit
- Wealth management
- Insurance
- Regulatory technology

#### Digital Assets
Treasury develops policy on:
- Cryptocurrencies
- Stablecoins
- Central bank digital currency (CBDC)
- Tokenized assets
- Decentralized finance (DeFi)

#### Payment Innovation
Modern payment trends including:
- Real-time payments
- Mobile payments
- Cross-border transactions
- Payment system interoperability

### Policy Objectives

Treasury balances multiple objectives:
- **Innovation** - Supporting beneficial innovation
- **Consumer Protection** - Protecting consumers and investors
- **Financial Stability** - Maintaining system stability
- **Illicit Finance** - Preventing misuse for crime
- **Competition** - Promoting fair competition

### Regulatory Approach

Treasury works with regulators on:
- Clear rules for new technologies
- Risk-based oversight
- Interagency coordination
- International harmonization

### Reports and Analysis

Treasury produces analysis on:
- Fintech ecosystem assessments
- Digital asset risks and opportunities
- Payment system modernization
- Artificial intelligence in finance

### International Engagement

Treasury engages internationally through:
- G7 and G20 working groups
- Financial Stability Board
- FATF on virtual assets
- Bilateral discussions
