## Financial Literacy and Education Commission (FLEC)

The Financial Literacy and Education Commission (FLEC) coordinates the federal government's efforts to promote financial literacy and education across America.

### About FLEC

Established by the Fair and Accurate Credit Transactions Act of 2003, FLEC:
- Coordinates federal financial literacy efforts
- Develops the National Strategy for Financial Literacy
- Promotes financial education best practices
- Supports research on financial capability

### Member Agencies

FLEC includes more than 20 federal agencies, including:
- Department of the Treasury (Chair)
- Consumer Financial Protection Bureau
- Department of Education
- Federal Reserve Board
- Securities and Exchange Commission
- Department of Labor

### National Strategy

FLEC develops the National Strategy for Financial Literacy, which:
- Sets goals for improving financial literacy
- Identifies priority populations
- Coordinates federal resources
- Measures progress and outcomes

### Key Focus Areas

#### Youth Financial Education
- K-12 financial education integration
- College financial wellness programs
- Young adult money management

#### Adult Financial Capability
- Workplace financial education
- Retirement planning
- Managing credit and debt

#### Underserved Populations
- Low-income households
- Military families
- Limited English proficiency communities
- Persons with disabilities

### MyMoney.gov

FLEC maintains [MyMoney.gov](https://www.mymoney.gov/), the federal government's financial education website, covering:
- Earning
- Saving and investing
- Protecting
- Spending
- Borrowing
