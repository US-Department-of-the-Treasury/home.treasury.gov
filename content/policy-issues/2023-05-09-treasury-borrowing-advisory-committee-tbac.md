The TBAC is an advisory committee governed by federal statute (the[Federal Advisory Committee Act](https://www.gsa.gov/policy-regulations/policy/federal-advisory-committee-management/legislation-and-regulations/the-federal-advisory-committee-act)and the[Government Securities Act)](https://treasurydirect.gov/files/laws-and-regulations/gsa/gsa-reg-gsr-pl-103-202.pdf)that meets quarterly with the Treasury Department. The TBAC's membership is comprised of senior representatives from a variety of buy and sell side institutions, such as banks, broker-dealers, asset managers, hedge funds, and insurance companies. The TBAC presents their observations to the Treasury Department on the overall strength of the U.S. economy as well as providing recommendations on a variety of technical debt management issues.
