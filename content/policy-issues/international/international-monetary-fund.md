## International Monetary Fund

The U.S. Department of the Treasury leads U.S. engagement with the International Monetary Fund (IMF), the international organization responsible for promoting global monetary cooperation and financial stability.

### U.S. Role at the IMF

The United States is the largest shareholder in the IMF and:
- Holds approximately 17% of voting power
- Has the only single-country veto on major decisions
- Is represented on the IMF Executive Board
- Contributes to IMF resources

### Treasury's Responsibilities

Treasury:
- Sets U.S. policy toward the IMF
- Instructs the U.S. Executive Director
- Reports to Congress on IMF activities
- Manages U.S. financial participation

### IMF Functions

#### Surveillance
The IMF monitors:
- Global economic conditions
- Member country economies
- Exchange rate policies
- Financial sector stability

#### Lending
The IMF provides:
- Balance of payments support
- Emergency financing
- Concessional lending to low-income countries
- Special Drawing Rights (SDRs)

#### Technical Assistance
The IMF offers:
- Policy advice
- Capacity building
- Training for officials
- Data standards

### Congressional Oversight

Treasury reports to Congress on:
- U.S. participation in the IMF
- IMF lending programs
- Policy initiatives
- Governance reforms
