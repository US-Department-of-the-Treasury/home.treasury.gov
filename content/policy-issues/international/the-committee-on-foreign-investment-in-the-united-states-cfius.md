## The Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States (CFIUS)

CFIUS is an interagency committee that reviews certain transactions involving foreign investment in the United States to determine their effect on national security.

### About CFIUS

CFIUS is authorized to:
- Review foreign investments in U.S. businesses
- Identify national security risks
- Impose mitigation measures
- Recommend the President block transactions

### Member Agencies

CFIUS is chaired by Treasury and includes:
- Department of Defense
- Department of State
- Department of Commerce
- Department of Homeland Security
- Department of Justice
- Department of Energy
- Office of the U.S. Trade Representative
- Office of Science and Technology Policy

### Jurisdiction

CFIUS reviews "covered transactions" including:
- Mergers, acquisitions, or takeovers
- Investments that provide foreign control
- Certain non-controlling investments in sensitive businesses
- Real estate transactions near sensitive sites

### Review Process

1. **Filing** - Parties file voluntary or mandatory declarations/notices
2. **Review** - CFIUS conducts national security review (45 days)
3. **Investigation** - If needed, investigation period (45 additional days)
4. **Decision** - CFIUS clears, imposes conditions, or recommends presidential action

### FIRRMA

The Foreign Investment Risk Review Modernization Act (FIRRMA) expanded CFIUS jurisdiction to cover:
- Critical technology
- Critical infrastructure
- Sensitive personal data
- Real estate near sensitive facilities

### Mandatory Declarations

Certain transactions require mandatory filing:
- Foreign government-involved investments in critical technology
- Investments in TID U.S. businesses by certain investors
