The U.S. Department of the Treasury oversees U.S. participation in the multilateral development banks (MDBs), which provide financing for development in countries around the world.

### Key Institutions

#### World Bank Group
- International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (IBRD)
- International Development Association (IDA)
- International Finance Corporation (IFC)
- Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (MIGA)

#### Regional Development Banks
- Inter-American Development Bank (IDB)
- African Development Bank (AfDB)
- Asian Development Bank (ADB)
- European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD)

### U.S. Role

The United States is a major shareholder in MDBs and:
- Contributes capital and resources
- Influences policy and priorities
- Votes on major decisions
- Promotes U.S. values and interests

### Treasury's Responsibilities

Treasury:
- Sets U.S. policy toward MDBs
- Instructs U.S. Executive Directors
- Manages U.S. financial contributions
- Reports to Congress

### Development Priorities

U.S. policy priorities at MDBs include:
- Climate and clean energy
- Health and pandemic preparedness
- Food security
- Infrastructure investment
- Private sector development
- Good governance

### Congressional Authorization

U.S. participation in MDBs requires:
- Authorization of capital contributions
- Appropriation of funds
- Treasury reporting to Congress
