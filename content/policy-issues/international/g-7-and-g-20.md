## G-7 and G-20

The U.S. Department of the Treasury represents the United States in the G-7 and G-20 forums on international economic and financial issues.

### The G-7

The Group of Seven includes:
- United States
- Canada
- France
- Germany
- Italy
- Japan
- United Kingdom
- European Union (guest)

#### G-7 Finance Track
Finance Ministers and Central Bank Governors address:
- Global economic coordination
- Financial regulation
- International development
- Crisis response

### The G-20

The Group of Twenty includes the G-7 plus:
- Argentina
- Australia
- Brazil
- China
- India
- Indonesia
- Mexico
- Russia
- Saudi Arabia
- South Africa
- South Korea
- Turkey
- European Union

#### G-20 Finance Track
Finance Ministers and Central Bank Governors focus on:
- Global economic policy coordination
- Financial stability
- International financial architecture
- Development finance

### Key Issues

#### Economic Coordination
- Growth strategies
- Fiscal policy
- Monetary policy coordination
- Structural reforms

#### Financial Regulation
- Banking standards
- Non-bank finance
- Cyber resilience
- Climate financial risks

#### International Tax
- Corporate tax reform
- Global minimum tax
- Digital taxation
- Tax transparency

#### Development
- Sustainable development financing
- Debt relief
- Climate finance
- Infrastructure investment

### Meeting Structure

Annual G-7 and G-20 cycles include:
- Multiple finance minister meetings
- Central bank governor meetings
- Leaders' summits
- Working group sessions
