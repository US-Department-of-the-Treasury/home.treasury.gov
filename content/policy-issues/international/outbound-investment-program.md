## Outbound Investment Security Program

The U.S. Department of the Treasury administers the Outbound Investment Security Program, which addresses national security concerns arising from certain U.S. investments in countries of concern.

### Program Overview

The program requires U.S. persons to:
- Notify Treasury of certain outbound investments
- Refrain from certain prohibited investments
- Comply with program requirements

### Covered Transactions

The program covers investments by U.S. persons in:
- Semiconductors and microelectronics
- Quantum information technologies
- Artificial intelligence systems

### Countries of Concern

The program initially focuses on investments involving:
- People's Republic of China (including Hong Kong and Macau)

### Types of Coverage

#### Prohibited Transactions
Certain investments are prohibited based on:
- Technology sensitivity
- End use concerns
- National security implications

#### Notifiable Transactions
Other covered investments require:
- Advance notification to Treasury
- Information about the investment
- Description of covered activities

### Compliance Obligations

U.S. persons must:
- Conduct due diligence on potential investments
- Determine if transactions are covered
- File required notifications
- Maintain records

### Relationship to CFIUS

While CFIUS reviews inbound investment, the Outbound Investment Program addresses:
- Capital flowing from the United States
- Investments that could benefit adversaries
- Transfer of expertise and knowledge
