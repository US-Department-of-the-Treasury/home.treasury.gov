The U.S. Department of the Treasury publishes data on ownership of Treasury securities, providing insight into who holds U.S. government debt.

### Types of Investor Data

#### Foreign Holdings
- Major foreign holders of Treasury securities
- Monthly estimates by country
- Changes in foreign ownership over time

#### Domestic Holdings
- Federal Reserve holdings
- Commercial bank holdings
- Mutual fund holdings
- Individual investors

#### Investor Class Data
- Auction allotments by investor type
- Primary dealer participation
- Foreign and international monetary authority participation

### Major Data Sources

#### Treasury International Capital (TIC) System
The TIC system collects data on:
- Cross-border portfolio investment
- Foreign holdings of U.S. securities
- U.S. holdings of foreign securities
- Banking data on international transactions

[Learn more about TIC data](/data/treasury-international-capital-tic-system/)

#### Investor Class Auction Allotments
Auction results show the distribution of purchases among:
- Primary dealers
- Direct bidders
- Indirect bidders (including foreign investors)

[View Investor Class Auction Allotments](/data/investor-class-auction-allotments/)

### Key Statistics

Treasury investor data helps answer:
- Who are the largest holders of U.S. debt?
- How has foreign ownership changed over time?
- What is the composition of domestic holders?
- How do different investors participate in auctions?
