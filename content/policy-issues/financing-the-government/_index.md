Treasury is responsible for managing the federal government's finances, including borrowing to fund operations and managing the national debt.

## Key Responsibilities

### Debt Management
The Office of Debt Management issues Treasury securities to finance government operations at the [lowest cost over time](/policy-issues/financing-the-government/debt-management).

### Fiscal Operations
The Bureau of the Fiscal Service manages federal payments, collections, and accounting, serving as the government's central financial manager.

### Cash Management
Managing the government's daily cash position to ensure sufficient funds are available to meet obligations while minimizing borrowing costs.

### Financial Reporting
Producing the government's financial statements and promoting transparency in federal financial management.

## Treasury Securities

Treasury issues a variety of securities to meet federal borrowing needs:

- **Treasury Bills** - Short-term securities (4-52 weeks)
- **Treasury Notes** - Medium-term securities (2-10 years)
- **Treasury Bonds** - Long-term securities (20-30 years)
- **TIPS** - Inflation-protected securities
- **Savings Bonds** - Securities for individual investors
