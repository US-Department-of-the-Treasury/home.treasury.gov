**WASHINGTON **– On Tuesday, May 17, the U.S. Treasury Department will host a public meeting of the Community Development Advisory Board.  The Advisory Board provides advice to the Director of Treasury’s Community Development Financial Institutions Fund (CDFI Fund) on the policies and activities of the CDFI Fund which focuses on increasing economic opportunity and promoting investment in America’s low-income communities.  The meeting will include discussions on the development of a five-year strategic plan and the release of a new report on Access to Capital and Credit in Native Communities, among other topics.  Additional information, including the agenda and a press kit which includes the biographies of the CDFI Fund’s leadership and Advisory Board members is available at: [http://www.cdfifund.gov/cdab](http://www.cdfifund.gov/cdab).   **WHO**: Adrienne Harris, Special Assistant to the President for Economic Policy, National Economic Council, White House Rosie Rios, Treasurer of the Unites States (Advisory Board member) Jessica Milano, Deputy Assistant Secretary for Small Business, Community Development and Housing, Department of the Treasury Annie Donovan, Director, CDFI Fund, Department of the Treasury Community Development Advisory Board members **WHEN**: Tuesday, May 17, 2016 9:00 a.m. EDT   **WHERE**:              U.S. Treasury Department Cash Room 1500 Pennsylvania Avenue, NW Washington, DC    **RSVP**: Media planning to attend must RSVP to Ronda Buckmon at [Ronda.Buckmon@treasury.gov](mailto:Ronda.Buckmon@treasury.gov). Media without Treasury press credentials must include the following information: full legal name, Social Security number, date of birth, and country of citizenship.  **The deadline to RSVP is Monday, May 16 2:00 PM EDT.**   *Media attending with camera equipment should arrive at the South Entrance (on 15th Street and Alexander Hamilton Place) at 7:00 a.m. to allow time for equipment sweeps and set-up. No vehicles will be permitted onto Treasury grounds; equipment must be carried in. All other media should arrive by 8:30 a.m. at the North entrance (Pennsylvania Avenue and 15th Street).*   A live webcast of the open session will be available at: [https://www.home.treasury.gov/news/webcasts](https://home.treasury.gov/news/webcasts) ###
