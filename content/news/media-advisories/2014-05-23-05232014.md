​ WASHINGTON – On Thursday, May 29, 2014, Under Secretary for Domestic Finance Mary Miller will deliver opening remarks at public meeting of the Financial Literacy and Education Commission. Under Secretary Miller will be joined by Richard Cordray, Director of the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, who will also address the Commission during the opening session. During the meeting, Under Secretary Miller, Director Cordray, Treasury Acting Assistant Secretary for Financial Institutions Amias Gerety as well as other officials will discuss efforts to advance the financial capability of the nation’s young people.< Who:   Mary Miller, Under Secretary for Domestic Finance, U.S. Department of the Treasury    Richard Cordray, Director of the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau Amias Gerety, Acting Assistant Secretary, Acting Assistant Secretary for Financial Institutions, What: Remarks before a public meeting of the Financial Literacy and Education Commission (FLEC) Where: U.S. Department of the Treasury Cash Room 1500 Pennsylvania Avenue, NW Washington, DC 20220 When: Thursday, May 29, 2014 at 9:00 AM EST RSVP:  Interested media must RSVP by 1:00 p.m. on Wednesday, May 28, to Ronda Buckmon at [Ronda.Buckmon@treasury.gov](mailto:Ronda.Buckmon@treasury.gov).  Media without press credentials issued by Treasury must include the following information for access into the building: full legal name, date of birth, Social Security number, and country of citizenship.     Press Logistics: This event is open press. A live webcast will also be available here:[www.home.treasury.gov/news/webcasts](https://home.treasury.gov/news/webcasts). Media attending with camera equipment should arrive at the South Entrance (on 15th Street and Alexander Hamilton Avenue) no later than 7:45 a.m. to allow time for equipment sweeps and set-up. No vehicles will be permitted onto Treasury grounds; equipment must be carried in. All other media should arrive by 8:30 a.m. at the North entrance (Pennsylvania Avenue and 15th Street). ###
