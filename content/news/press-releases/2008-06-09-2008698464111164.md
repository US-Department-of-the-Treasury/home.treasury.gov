Secretary Henry M. Paulson, Jr. delivered remarks today on the U.S. – China economic relationship and the upcoming meeting of the Strategic Economic Dialogue (SED). Presidents Bush and Hu established the SED in 2006 to provide a focused and effective framework for addressing economic issues of mutual concern. The SED focuses on a range of issues including financial sector reform and increased currency flexibility, the integrity of trade and product safety, and investment. The fourth Cabinet level meeting of the U.S. – China Strategic Economic Dialogue is scheduled to take place at the U.S. Naval Academy in Annapolis, Md. on June 17 – 18, 2008.
