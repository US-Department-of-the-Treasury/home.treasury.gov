**Event is Part of Nationwide Outreach Initiative to Help Homeowners Avoid Foreclosure by Connecting them with their Mortgage Company and a HUD-Approved Housing Counselor**
 
**CHICAGO, IL** – On Monday, April 23 at 12:30 p.m., Deputy Secretary of the U.S. Treasury Department Neal Wolin and HOPE NOW Alliance Executive Director Faith Schwartz will join National Association of REALTORS® President Moe Veissi, NeighborWorks® America National Director of Homeownership and Lending Marietta Rodriguez and Illinois Housing Development Authority Executive Director Mary Kenney for a media availability to kick off the free Help for Homeowners Community Event at the Donald E. Stephens Convention Center in Rosemont. The Help for Homeowners Community Event is part of a nationwide outreach initiative by the Obama Administration, the HOPE NOW Alliance and NeighborWorks® America to provide free help for struggling homeowners by connecting them directly with their mortgage servicer and a HUD-approved housing counselor to discuss alternatives to foreclosure. Sixteen of the nation’s largest mortgage servicers and more than a dozen local HUD-approved housing counseling organizations are confirmed for Monday’s event to assist struggling homeowners in the Chicago area.

**ABOUT THE EVENT SPONSORS**
 
 
 
**The Obama Administration's Making Home Affordable Program** was created to help struggling homeowners modify or refinance their mortgage payments to make them more affordable, or transition to more affordable housing to prevent avoidable foreclosures. For more information, go to [www.MakingHomeAffordable.gov](http://www.makinghomeaffordable.gov/).
 
 
 
**HOPE NOW** is the industry-created alliance of mortgage servicers, investors, counselors, and other mortgage market participants, brought together by the Financial Services Roundtable, Housing Policy Council and Mortgage Bankers Association, that has developed and is implementing a coordinated plan to help as many homeowners as possible prevent foreclosure and stay in their homes. For more information, go to [www.hopenow.com](http://www.hopenow.com/).
