The Troubled Assets Relief Program (TARP) was created in 2008 to stabilize the financial system during the financial crisis. This page provides access to TARP data and reports.

### Program Overview

TARP was authorized by the Emergency Economic Stabilization Act of 2008:
- Initial authorization of up to $700 billion
- Later reduced to $475 billion
- Programs to stabilize banks, housing, and auto industry

### Major TARP Programs

#### Capital Purchase Program (CPP)
- Investments in banking institutions
- Preferred stock purchases
- Stabilized the banking system

#### Automotive Industry Financing
- Support for GM and Chrysler
- Auto supplier support
- Preserved manufacturing jobs

#### Making Home Affordable
- Home Affordable Modification Program (HAMP)
- Hardest Hit Fund
- Housing Finance Agency programs

### TARP Reports

Treasury published regular TARP reports:
- Monthly 105(a) Reports to Congress
- TARP Financials
- Transaction reports
- Investment details

### Program Status

TARP investment programs have concluded:
- Most investments have been repaid
- Program returned positive to taxpayers
- Final accounting in Treasury reports

### Oversight

TARP was subject to extensive oversight:
- Special Inspector General for TARP (SIGTARP)
- Congressional Oversight Panel
- Government Accountability Office (GAO)
- Financial Stability Oversight Board
