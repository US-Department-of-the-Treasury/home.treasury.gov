## TARP Housing Programs

The Troubled Assets Relief Program (TARP) included several programs aimed at helping homeowners avoid foreclosure during and after the financial crisis.

### Making Home Affordable (MHA)

The Making Home Affordable initiative included:

#### Home Affordable Modification Program (HAMP)
- Reduced monthly mortgage payments
- Modified loan terms for struggling homeowners
- Required servicer participation

#### Home Affordable Refinance Program (HARP)
- Refinancing for underwater homeowners
- Enabled refinancing above loan-to-value limits
- Reduced monthly payments

#### Other MHA Programs
- Principal Reduction Alternative (PRA)
- Home Affordable Unemployment Program (UP)
- Second Lien Modification Program (2MP)

### Hardest Hit Fund (HHF)

The Hardest Hit Fund provided assistance to states with:
- Highest unemployment
- Steepest home price declines

HHF programs included:
- Mortgage payment assistance
- Principal reduction
- Transition assistance
- Blight elimination

### Housing Finance Agency (HFA) Programs

Support for state housing finance agencies:
- HFA Hardest Hit Fund
- New Issue Bond Program
- Temporary Credit and Liquidity Program

### Program Results

TARP housing programs:
- Helped millions of homeowners
- Reduced foreclosures
- Stabilized housing markets
- Programs have concluded

### Reports and Data

TARP housing data was reported in:
- Monthly servicer reports
- MHA Program Performance Reports
- State-level Hardest Hit Fund reports
- Final program assessments
