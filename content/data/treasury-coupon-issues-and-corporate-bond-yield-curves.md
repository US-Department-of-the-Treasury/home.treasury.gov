## Treasury Coupon Issues and Corporate Bond Yield Curves

The U.S. Department of the Treasury publishes data on Treasury coupon issues and corporate bond yield curves, which are used for various financial and regulatory purposes.

### Treasury Coupon Issues

Treasury coupon issues include:
- Treasury Notes (2-10 year maturities)
- Treasury Bonds (20-30 year maturities)
- TIPS (Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities)

#### Historical Data
Treasury maintains historical records of:
- Issue dates and maturity dates
- Coupon rates
- Outstanding amounts
- CUSIP numbers

### Corporate Bond Yield Curves

Treasury publishes corporate bond yield curves for use in pension funding calculations under ERISA.

#### High Quality Corporate Bond Yield Curve
The 24-month average high quality corporate bond yield curve is used for:
- Single-employer defined benefit plan funding
- Lump sum calculations
- Present value determinations

#### Segment Rates
The curve is used to derive:
- First segment rate (0-5 years)
- Second segment rate (5-20 years)
- Third segment rate (20+ years)

### Pension Funding Applications

These rates are critical for:
- Minimum funding requirements
- Maximum contribution deductions
- Lump sum distribution calculations
- PBGC variable rate premiums

### Monthly Updates

Treasury updates the corporate bond yield curve monthly, published in:
- IRS notices
- Treasury announcements
- Federal Register (as applicable)
