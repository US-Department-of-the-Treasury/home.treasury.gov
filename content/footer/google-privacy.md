The U.S. Department of the Treasury uses Google Analytics to help understand how visitors use our website.

### What Google Analytics Collects

Google Analytics collects:
- Pages you visit on our site
- Time spent on pages
- How you arrived at our site
- Your general geographic location (city/region level)
- Browser and device information

### What Google Analytics Does NOT Collect

We have configured Google Analytics to NOT collect:
- Your name or personal identity
- Your full IP address (we use IP anonymization)
- Information that could identify you personally

### How We Use This Data

We use Google Analytics data to:
- Understand which pages are most popular
- Identify areas for improvement
- Measure the effectiveness of content
- Improve user experience

### Opting Out

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by:
- Installing the [Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout)
- Disabling cookies in your browser settings
- Using your browser's "Do Not Track" feature

### Google's Privacy Practices

For more information about Google's privacy practices, see:
- [Google Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy)
- [How Google Uses Data](https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites)
