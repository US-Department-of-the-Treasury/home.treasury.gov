## Freedom of Information Act (FOIA)

The Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) provides the public with the right to request access to records from any federal agency.

### Submitting a FOIA Request

You may submit a FOIA request to Treasury by:

**Online**
Submit requests through the Treasury FOIA portal

**Mail**
FOIA Request
Disclosure Services
U.S. Department of the Treasury
1500 Pennsylvania Avenue, NW
Washington, DC 20220

**Email**
FOIAOffice@treasury.gov

### What to Include

Your FOIA request should include:
- A clear description of the records you seek
- Time frame for the records
- Your contact information
- Preferred format for receiving records

### Response Times

Treasury strives to respond to FOIA requests within:
- 20 business days for standard requests
- 10 business days for expedited processing (if granted)

Complex requests may take longer.

### Fees

FOIA requests may incur fees for:
- Search time
- Review time
- Duplication costs

Fee waivers may be available for certain requesters.

### Appeals

If your request is denied, you may:
- Submit an administrative appeal within 90 days
- Seek judicial review in federal court

### FOIA Library

Treasury posts frequently requested records in its FOIA reading room to provide proactive disclosure of government information.

### Contact

For questions about FOIA requests, contact the Treasury FOIA Office.
